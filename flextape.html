The F/A-18 Hornet

History of F/A-18

The F/A-18A was a development of the YF-17. The F/A-18A's first flight was on the 18th of November in 1978. It was approved for full production by the U.S. Military in 1983.

The F/A-18C begin being manufactued in september 1987 and is still used by the USMC today.

Radar

The F/A-18A/B variants were equipt with the AN/APG-65 radar.

The F/A-18C/D variant's radars were replaced and the C/Ds were given the AN/APG-73. This same radar is present in proceeding variants of the F/A-18.

Cockpit

The cockpit of the F/A-18 has 3 screens, the lower one of which shows navigational data. The other two are hooked to the onboard computer which allows the pilot to make them display whatever they need in that moment, such as the radar, weapon selection, or the targeting pod.

Controls

Every F/A-18 is control by the computer from the pilot's joystick input. That is to say that without power, you have no ability to fly or glide.

Every other Hornet variant (B,D and F) are dual-seat aircraft with the pilot up front and a pilot filling a WSO role directly behind them.

Engines

The F/A-18 Hornet has two twin General Electric F404 afterburning turbofan engines. These engines along with the weight of the aircraft allows it to take off with a gross weight of 51900lbs. Its typical deployment T/O weight is 44000lbs for comparison, and 24000lbs when just the aircraft and fuel is weighed.
